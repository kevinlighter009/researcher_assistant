# OmniDrive: A Holistic Vision-Language Dataset for Autonomous Driving with Counterfactual Reasoning

## Keywords
- vla, mllm, counterfactual-reasoning, q-former, 3d-perception, drivelm, nuscenes

## TL;DR
Existing driving VLM datasets supervise only on safe expert trajectories, providing sparse signal that does not teach a model why alternative actions would be unsafe. The authors build OmniDrive, a counterfactual-based 3D Q&A dataset on nuScenes generated by simulating alternate trajectories, scoring them with a rule-based traffic-rule checklist, and prompting GPT-4 to produce scene descriptions, attention, counterfactual reasoning and decision/planning Q&A. They release two baselines, Omni-L (LLaVA-style MLP projector) and Omni-Q (StreamPETR-style BEV Q-Former), and report improvements on the DriveLM benchmark (score 0.53 to 0.58 with OmniDrive + LLaVA665k pre-training) and on nuScenes open-loop planning.

## Problem & Motivation
Prior driving LLM-agent datasets (DriveLM, LingoQA, NuScenes-QA) mostly rely on expert log-replay trajectories, giving only sparse supervision tied to a single safe action and avoiding the deeper "what if I did otherwise" reasoning that humans use. Two further gaps are addressed: (1) extending 2D VLM understanding to full 3D geometric/spatial reasoning needed for driving, and (2) the lack of a principled comparison between integrating 3D perception stacks into VLMs versus migrating 2D VLMs to 3D. The paper argues that counterfactual reasoning over simulated trajectories provides denser supervision linking planning and language, and that long-context 3D scenes are too complex for GPT-4 to handle from raw inputs without trajectory-centered structuring.

## Innovation Points
- **Counterfactual-centric Q&A pipeline** — simulate alternative trajectories per scene, validate them against a rule-based checklist (collisions, red lights, drivable area, lane topology) using nuScenes + OpenLane-v2 annotations, then prompt GPT-4 to produce structured Q&A; turns sparse expert supervision into dense planning-grounded language.
- **Planning-oriented key-frame selection** — two-stage K-means in CLIP semantic space and then in trajectory space (200 cluster centers) to cover diverse driving behaviors before mass data generation.
- **Trajectory-centered scene representation for GPT-4** — feeds expert trajectory, "close objects" within 10 m / 3 s, and stitched front/rear views to GPT-4 to overcome long-context 3D scene confusion.
- **Two contrasting agent designs** — Omni-L (LLaVA MLP projector with 3D positional encoding on flattened multi-view features) versus Omni-Q (StreamPETR-style 3D BEV Q-Former with carrier + perception queries), enabling head-to-head comparison of "2D VLM lifted to 3D" vs "3D perception stack plugged into LLM".
- **Carrier + perception query split in Omni-Q** — dedicated carrier queries align with LLM token dimension (4096) while perception queries supervise 3D detection, sharing self-attention so language gets geometric priors.

## Model Architecture
Two frameworks share a vision encoder and an LLaMA2-7B LLM, and differ only in the projector.

- **Shared frontend**: 6 surround cameras → EVA-02-L vision encoder (CLIP-distilled) → multi-view image features F_m of shape (N x C x H x W) → 3D positional encoding P_m added.
- **Omni-L (MLP projector, LLaVA-style)**: linear -> GELU -> linear flattens multi-view features into LLM token sequence; 3D PE weights initialized to zero for training stability.
- **Omni-Q (Q-Former, StreamPETR-style BEV)**: 6 transformer blocks. Carrier queries Q_c and detection queries Q_d are concatenated and run through hybrid self-attention (Eq. 1), then cross-attend to (P_m + F_m) (Eq. 2). Q_d predicts foreground 3D detections; Q_c is MLP-aligned to LLM token dimension and fed to LLaMA2-7B for text generation.
- **Training**: two stages, 2D pretraining (init Q-Former / MLP projector with LLaVA recipe) then 3D fine-tuning on driving Q&A; only text-generation loss (no BLIP-2 contrastive/matching). Optimizer AdamW, batch 16, projector lr 4e-4, vision encoder + LLM lr 2e-5, cosine annealing.
- **Output**: free-form text answers covering scene description, attention, counterfactual reasoning, decision making, and planning trajectories expressed as point sequences in BEV coordinates.
- **Dataset scale**: built on the 700 nuScenes training scenes, ~0.3 M image-question pairs (DriveLM scale, OmniDrive's exact size not reported beyond "large-scale").

## Benchmark Results
Headline: pre-training the agent on OmniDrive raises DriveLM final score from 0.53 (DriveLM only) to 0.58 (DriveLM + OmniDrive + LLaVA665k).

**DriveLM Q&A (Table 3):**
| Pre-training        | Acc  | CG   | BLEU | Rouge-L | CIDEr | Match | Score |
|---------------------|------|------|------|---------|-------|-------|-------|
| DriveLM only        | 0.60 | 0.65 | 0.50 | 0.71    | 0.07  | 0.36  | 0.53  |
| + OmniDrive         | 0.70 | 0.65 | 0.52 | 0.73    | 0.13  | 0.37  | 0.56  |
| + LLaVA665k         | 0.76 | 0.63 | 0.53 | 0.73    | 0.15  | 0.37  | 0.57  |
| **+ Both**          | **0.78** | 0.64 | 0.54 | 0.73 | 0.15 | 0.37 | **0.58** |

**nuScenes open-loop planning, Omni-Q++ (with ego status + planner cue), avg over 1/2/3 s (Table 2):**
| Method        | L2 (m) avg ↓ | Collision % avg ↓ | Intersection % avg ↓ |
|---------------|--------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| UniAD (ego)   | 0.46         | 0.37              | 1.59                 |
| VAD-Base (ego)| 0.37         | **0.67**(table value) | 2.47           |
| BEV-Planner++ | 0.35         | 0.34              | 3.16                 |
| **Omni-Q++**  | **0.33**     | **0.30**          | 3.00                 |

Omni-L++ (with ego status) reports L2 avg 0.40, collision 0.35, intersection 2.45.

**Counterfactual reasoning + open-loop planning, no ego status (Table 5):**
| Variant | Counterfactual AP | AR | Language CIDEr | Col % | Inter % |
|---------|-------------------|-----|----------------|-------|---------|
| **Omni-L**  | **53.7** | **63.0** | **73.2** | 1.90 | 3.29 |
| Omni-Q  | 52.3 | 59.6 | 68.6 | 3.79 | 4.59 |
| BEV-MLP | 45.6 | 49.5 | 59.5 | 4.43 | 8.56 |

Ablations (Table 4/5): removing OpenLane-v2 lane supervision in Omni-Q drops counterfactual AP from 52.3 to 51.2; further removing 3D object supervision drops it to 48.9 and worsens collision rate to 6.77%. Without OmniDrive Q&A, training only on trajectory prediction collapses Omni-L's distribution-modeling ability (collision 3.22% vs 1.90% with OmniDrive).

Authors' takeaway: migrating 2D VLMs to 3D (Omni-L) outperforms plugging 3D perception stacks into VLMs (Omni-Q) on language-heavy and counterfactual tasks, while Omni-Q wins when ego status is provided due to stronger geometric priors.

## Limitations & Open Questions
- Counterfactual outcomes are simulated as single-agent trajectory replays; reactions of other agents are not modeled, limiting realism of the "what if" supervision (acknowledged in Section 6).
- Evaluation is open-loop only (nuScenes, DriveLM); no closed-loop / CARLA test, so the true planning gain is unverified.
- LLMs are observed to overfit to ego status more easily than baselines, making fair "ego vs no-ego" comparisons tricky and arguably inflating headline planning numbers.
- Total dataset size, GPT-4 generation cost, inference latency of the 7B LLM agent, and TensorRT deployment numbers are not reported in the paper text.
- Omni-Q's weaker language ability is attributed to "pre-training gaps"; how to align traditional 3D perception stacks with language space remains an explicit open question.
