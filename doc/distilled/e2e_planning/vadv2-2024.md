# VADv2: End-to-End Vectorized Autonomous Driving via Probabilistic Planning

## Keywords
- probabilistic-planning, planning-vocabulary, trajectory-tokenization, vectorized-scene, carla-town05, navsim, bench2drive

## TL;DR
Existing learning-based end-to-end planners regress a single trajectory deterministically and fail when the feasible solution space is non-convex (e.g. yield vs. overtake). VADv2 reformulates planning as a scene-conditioned probability distribution over a 4096-action discrete planning vocabulary obtained by furthest-trajectory sampling from human demonstrations, and trains a transformer to predict per-action probabilities supervised by a KL-style cross-entropy loss plus a conflict loss. On CARLA Town05 Long it reaches DS 85.1 (vs. DriveMLM 76.1) using cameras only.

## Problem & Motivation
Prior end-to-end planners (UniAD, VAD, Transfuser, DriveMLM) regress one optimal future trajectory or low-level control. The authors argue this is misspecified because:
- Human demonstrations contain genuine multi-modality (e.g. lane-keep vs. lane-change, yield vs. overtake) that a deterministic regression target averages away.
- The feasible action set is often non-convex, so a regressed "mean" trajectory can produce an unsafe intermediate action.
- Diffusion-based or LLM-based alternatives either depend on ground-truth perception/HD maps (Diffusion Planner) or have impractical inference cost (LLM planners).
VADv2 instead models p(a|o), enabling multi-modal output, uncertainty as a confidence signal, and easy combination with rule-based wrappers.

## Innovation Points
- **Probabilistic planning formulation** — models planning as a scene-conditioned non-stationary stochastic process p(a|o) instead of regression; outputs a distribution over the entire action vocabulary, supervised by KL/cross-entropy against the empirical demonstration distribution.
- **Planning vocabulary via furthest trajectory sampling** — discretizes the continuous trajectory space by selecting N=4096 representative whole trajectories from real demonstrations using furthest-point sampling on endpoints; each token is a complete physically feasible 3-second trajectory (T=6 waypoints, 0.5 s spacing), avoiding the rollout error of step-wise tokenizers like MotionLM.
- **Probabilistic field encoding of actions** — each candidate trajectory is NeRF-style positionally encoded waypoint-by-waypoint into a planning token, so nearby trajectories receive similar embeddings and the learned probability is continuous in action space.
- **Conflict loss** — vocabulary actions that intersect predicted agent futures or road boundaries receive an explicit log-probability penalty, injecting driving priors as negative samples.
- **Vectorized scene tokens with traffic-light tokens** — scene encoder emits map, agent, traffic-element, and image tokens that interact with planning tokens via cross-attention in a cascaded transformer decoder; traffic-light tokenization is essential for CARLA closed-loop performance.

## Model Architecture
- Inputs: multi-view surround camera sequence (streaming, 10 Hz simulation/control), navigation info, ego state. No LiDAR, no HD map at inference.
- Scene encoder: BEVFormer-style BEV encoder produces a BEV feature map; from it the model derives four sets of scene tokens E_scene (M x D, D=256):
  - Map tokens (MapTRv2-style; lane centerlines, dividers, road boundaries, crossings)
  - Agent tokens (UniAD-style; location, orientation, size, speed, K future trajectories with min-FDE selection)
  - Traffic-element tokens (traffic light + stop sign, MLP head from front-view features)
  - Image tokens (front-view dense features as a complement)
- Navigation and ego state encoded into E_navi and E_state by MLPs.
- Action vocabulary V = {a_i}, |V|=4096, built offline by furthest-trajectory sampling from collected demonstrations; each a is a 6-waypoint trajectory.
- Planning token: each candidate trajectory waypoint pair (x_i, y_i) is positionally encoded via NeRF-style sin/cos at L frequencies, producing E(a) in R^{1xD}.
- Planning head: cascaded transformer decoder phi with E(a) as queries and E_scene as K/V, then p(a) = sigmoid(MLP(phi(E(a), E_scene) + E_navi + E_state)).
- Output: probability per vocabulary action; at inference, sample top-1 (or top-K with rule-based wrapper / optimization post-solver) and convert to steer/throttle/brake via PID.
- Training losses: distribution loss (cross-entropy vs. occurrence frequency where the per-frame label is the vocabulary action with min L2 to GT), conflict loss (penalize colliding/off-road vocabulary actions), and scene-token losses (map, agent, traffic-element supervision).
- Training scale (CARLA): ~3 M clips collected on Town03/04/06/07/10 with the official CARLA agent; 16 NVIDIA 4090 GPUs.

## Benchmark Results

**CARLA Town05 Long (closed-loop, camera-only for VADv2):**
| Method      | Modality | DS up | RC up | IS up |
|-------------|----------|-------|-------|-------|
| DriveMLM    | C+L      | 76.1  | 98.1  | 0.78  |
| LeapVAD     | C        | 73.7  | 95.7  | 0.78  |
| **VADv2**   | C        | **85.1** | **98.4** | **0.87** |

VADv2 surpasses DriveMLM (camera+LiDAR) by 9.0 DS using only cameras, and the prior best camera-only method (Rao et al. 2024, DS 74.9) by 10.2.

**Bench2Drive (closed-loop):** VADv2 DS 76.15, SR 50.46 - highest reported in Table 5 (vs. ETA 74.33 / 48.33).

**NAVSIM navtest (closed-loop metrics):** VADv2 PDMS 89.3 vs. Hydra-NeXt 88.6, DiffusionDrive 88.1, UniAD 83.4. VADv2 is camera-only (C) while most competitors are C+L.

**NAVSIMv2 (extended metrics, EPDMS):** VADv2 EPDMS 85.8 vs. PRIX 84.2, HydraMDP++ 81.4, Transfuser 76.7.

**3DGS-based real-data benchmark (337 reconstructed scenes, lower is better):** VADv2 CR 0.270 vs. Transfuser 0.320 (15.6% relative reduction); DR 0.243 competitive.

**Ablations:**
- Removing the Distribution Loss (Table 7, ID 1) collapses planning: L2@3s rises from 0.290 m to 3.153 m and collision@3s from 3.9% to 74.6%.
- Removing the Conflict Loss (ID 2) raises collision@3s from 3.9% to 8.5%.
- Replacing probabilistic head with a deterministic MLP regressor and stratifying by traffic density (Table 8): probabilistic beats deterministic at every density and is more stable - High-density PDMS 87.7 vs. 85.8, Low-density 90.6 vs. 89.4.
- Top-K candidate quality (Table 6): top-1 PDMS 89.3, top-5 still 87.5, indicating multi-modal outputs are individually viable.

## Limitations & Open Questions
- Authors acknowledge that current simulator and 3DGS closed-loop environments still feature naive agent behaviors and limited scene realism, which may cap VADv2's measured ceiling.
- Vocabulary is built once offline by furthest-point sampling on endpoints; how to grow / adapt the 4096 anchors online or per-region is not addressed, and longer horizons or higher waypoint counts would explode the discretization.
- Conflict loss requires predicted-agent futures and map boundaries during training, so mislabelled or noisy auxiliary supervision could systematically penalize valid trajectories.
- Compared to LLM/VLM planners, VADv2 has no explicit semantic reasoning channel for long-tail intent (gestures, signage); future work flagged is leveraging large-scale expert data to bridge sim-to-real.
- Inference compute and latency for the 4096-token cross-attention head are not reported.
