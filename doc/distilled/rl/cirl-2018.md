# CIRL: Controllable Imitative Reinforcement Learning for Vision-based Self-driving

## Keywords
- ddpg, imitation-learning, carla, conditional-imitation, command-gating, actor-critic, vision-based-driving

## TL;DR
Vision-based urban driving via pure imitation learning is bounded by demonstration coverage, while pure deep RL (e.g. A3C) explodes in sample complexity over the continuous steer/throttle/brake action space. CIRL first pretrains a conditional imitation network with a command-gated multi-branch head, then transfers its weights into a DDPG actor and continues training with per-command shaped rewards (abnormal steer-angle penalty, speed reward, collision/lane penalties). On the CARLA benchmark, CIRL reaches 82% success on Nav.dynamic in New town/weather vs 44% (MP) and 42% (IL), trained in roughly 12 hours of simulation rather than the ~12 days reported for A3C.

## Problem & Motivation
End-to-end imitation learning (e.g. Bojarski et al., Codevilla et al.) maps front-camera images to control commands but cannot generalize beyond the human demonstration distribution and fails on long-horizon, goal-oriented navigation that requires interaction with other vehicles, pedestrians, and roadway geometry. Conversely, deep RL applied directly to driving (Sallab et al., the A3C baseline in CARLA) suffers from the high-dimensional continuous action space — random exploration is so unproductive that prior RL pipelines required ~12 days of CARLA simulation with 10 parallel actors and still produced unsatisfactory results. CIRL targets this exploration bottleneck while preserving the goal-conditioning needed for navigation.

## Innovation Points
- **Imitative DDPG warm start** — the DDPG actor is initialized from a conditional imitation policy pretrained on 14 hours of human CARLA driving, replacing random exploration seeds with a "good starting point" so OU-noise exploration refines a competent policy instead of bootstrapping one from scratch.
- **Command gating with four branches** — a categorical command c in {Follow, Straight, TurnLeft, TurnRight} routes the shared backbone into one of four specialized heads predicting (steer, throttle, brake), enabling controllability by an upstream planner/driver intent and per-command specialization.
- **Per-command abnormal steer-angle reward** — explicit penalties (-15 for steering opposite to TurnLeft/TurnRight, -20 for |s|>0.2 under Straight) give command-aware action feedback that stabilizes RL fine-tuning, beyond the CARLA-default reward terms.
- **First successful deep-RL pipeline on CARLA** — the authors claim the first RL-trained vision-only driving policy that beats the modular pipeline and supervised imitation on the CARLA benchmark.
- **Demonstration-as-initializer (vs. demonstration-as-replay)** — direct comparison with DDPGfD shows that loading demos into the replay buffer ("CIRL w/ add reply") gives no measurable gain over CIRL's pure parameter-init scheme on this task.

## Model Architecture
- Input: forward-facing camera image, scalar speed measurement, and a categorical command c from the topological planner.
- Backbone: 8-layer conv net (channels 32-32-64-64-128-128-128-128) reducing 200x88 input to a 512-d feature, concatenated with a 128-d speed embedding.
- Command gating G(c) selectively activates one of four 256-256-3 branches; output action a = (steer_angle, acceleration, braking), all continuous.
- Stage 1 (Controllable Imitation Learning): minimize weighted L2 loss between predicted and ground-truth (s, a, b) over N driving sequences.
- Stage 2 (Imitative DDPG): actor F^pi is initialized from theta^I; critic F^Q shares the backbone and ingests (image, speed, action) -> per-command value head. Standard DDPG loss with target networks; OU exploration noise (mu in {0, 0.15, 0.5}, sigma in {0.02, 0.05, 0}) for steer/throttle/brake.
- Reward r = r_s (per-command steer penalty) + r_v (per-command speed reward, capped at 25/35/20 km/h depending on c) + r_r (-100 sidewalk overlap) + r_o (-100 opposite-lane overlap) + r_d (-100 vehicle/pedestrian collision, -50 other).
- Training: gamma 0.9, actor LR 1e-5, critic LR 1e-3, linearly decayed; ~0.3M simulation steps (~12 h on 4x GTX 1080) vs 10M steps (~12 days) for the A3C baseline. TensorFlow.

## Benchmark Results
**CARLA goal-directed navigation, % success (higher is better), one shared agent over four tasks:**

| Task         | Cond.       | MP  | IL  | RL (A3C) | CIRL    |
|--------------|-------------|-----|-----|----------|---------|
| Straight     | Train       | 98  | 95  | 89       | **98**  |
| One turn     | Train       | 82  | 89  | 34       | **97**  |
| Navigation   | Train       | 80  | 86  | 14       | **93**  |
| Nav. dynamic | Train       | 77  | **83** | 7    | 82      |
| Straight     | New town    | 92  | 97  | 74       | **100** |
| One turn     | New town    | 61  | 59  | 12       | **71**  |
| Navigation   | New town    | 24  | 40  | 3        | **53**  |
| Nav. dynamic | New town    | 24  | 38  | 2        | **41**  |
| Nav. dynamic | New town/weather | 44 | 42 | 4    | **62**  |

Generalization on four further unseen settings (Table 2): Nav.dynamic 47/86/87/68 across New town/path2, New weather2, New path, New town/weather2.

Ablations on One-turn (Table 4):
- CIRL w/o steer reward vs CIRL: 91/65/96/76 vs 97/71/94/82 -> the per-command abnormal-steer reward helps in 3 of 4 settings.
- CIRL w/ add reply (DDPGfD-style replay) vs CIRL: ~96/71/94/82 vs 97/71/94/82 -> demonstration replay buffer adds nothing material.
- 0.5M-step CIRL vs default: no significant gain from extra simulation steps.

Real-scene transfer (Comma.ai, Table 5) — steer-angle MAE: PilotNet 1.208, CIRL (CARLA, zero-shot) 2.939, CIRL from scratch 1.186, **CIRL finetuning 1.168** (best).

## Limitations & Open Questions
- Performance collapses on heavy-rain weather (HardRainSunset Nav. in new town: 5% success, Table 3) — perception fails when the road is occluded by rain.
- Single front camera only; no LiDAR, no surround view, no temporal/recurrent context — limits applicability to denser urban scenes.
- "Demonstrations as init only" works on CARLA but is not contrasted with longer training of DDPGfD; superiority over replay-based use of demos may not generalize beyond this benchmark.
- CARLA 0.8.x simulator with hand-designed reward shaping (collision/lane penalties, capped speed targets); reward scales follow the Coach RL framework and would need re-tuning per environment.
- Closed-loop only inside CARLA; real-world transfer is evaluated only as open-loop steer-angle regression on Comma.ai, not closed-loop driving.
