# Model-Based Imitation Learning for Urban Driving

## Keywords
- model-based-il, world-model, latent-dynamics, bev-segmentation, carla, offline-imitation, driving-in-imagination

## TL;DR
Prior model-based methods for driving rely on online environment interaction and a reward signal, while pure imitation learning suffers from compounding errors and cannot reason about scene dynamics. The authors propose MILE, which jointly learns a probabilistic latent world model (static scene + dynamic agents + ego behaviour) and a driving policy from a fixed offline corpus of expert CARLA logs, using 3D-lifted camera features pooled to BEV as an inductive bias. MILE achieves a 61.1 driving score in a new town under new weather on CARLA — a 31% relative improvement over LAV — while using only RGB input, and can drive purely from imagined latent rollouts.

## Problem & Motivation
Prior end-to-end driving from images via pure imitation (e.g. CILRS, LBC, TransFuser) struggles to model scene dynamics and is brittle to compounding errors. Classical model-based RL with world models (Dreamer, PlaNet) requires a reward function and online interaction with the environment — neither is available in real driving. Earlier world models also assume the agent does not influence the environment and are evaluated on tiny 64×64 visual inputs (Atari, MuJoCo). Driving needs (i) high-resolution RGB input so small features like traffic lights are visible, (ii) action-conditioned dynamics because other agents respond to the ego, and (iii) a fully offline training regime. MILE targets all three.

## Innovation Points
- **Joint model + policy from offline expert data only** — no reward, no online env interaction, no LiDAR; trained on 2.9M frames (32 h) of CARLA expert demonstrations.
- **3D-geometry inductive bias** — image features are lifted to 3D via predicted depth distributions and sum-pooled into a BEV grid before being compressed to a 1D 512-d latent; ablations show this is critical.
- **Compact 1D latent for world dynamics** — bottleneck is a 512-d vector (not a spatial tensor), trained with a recurrent prior/posterior matched by a KL term à la RSSM; ablations show higher-dim spatial latents hurt closed-loop driving.
- **Action-conditioned latent dynamics** — relaxes the "agent does not affect environment" assumption of recent world models, so the model captures how other agents react to ego actions.
- **Driving in imagination** — the policy can roll out actions purely from imagined latent states (no observations) and execute complex manoeuvres (roundabouts, swerving) entirely from internal predictions.
- **Fully recurrent deployment** — at inference the latent state is updated incrementally instead of recomputed from scratch each step, giving 43.0 Hz vs. 6.2 Hz for the reset-state baseline at no driving-performance cost.

## Model Architecture
- Inputs at 5 Hz: single forward RGB camera (3×600×960), a 64×64 grayscale route map (navigation goal), scalar speed, and 2D action (acceleration, steering); BeV semantic segmentation (8 classes) used as supervision only.
- Observation encoder e_φ: image encoder → predicted depth distribution → lift features to 3D voxels using camera K, M (Lift-Splat) → sum-pool to BEV grid → conv backbone → 1D image feature x'_t ∈ R^{C'}; concatenated with route-map and speed features into x_t ∈ R^{512}.
- Latent dynamics: deterministic history h_t updated by a gated recurrent cell f_θ; stochastic state s_t modelled as a diagonal Gaussian. Posterior q(s_t | o_≤t, a_<t) (inference net) and prior p(s_t | h_{t-1}, s_{t-1}) (generative net) are matched by a KL term.
- Decoders (generative): image decoder g_θ (StyleGAN-like, AdaIN conditioned on latent) → reconstructed RGB; BeV decoder l_θ → categorical 192×192 segmentation; policy π_θ (MLP) → Laplace-distributed action (so action loss is L1).
- ELBO loss = image reconstruction (MSE) + BeV cross-entropy + action L1 + KL(posterior || prior).
- Trained 50k iterations, batch 64, sequence length T=12, AdamW lr 1e-4, weight decay 0.01, on 8× V100.

## Benchmark Results
**CARLA, new town (Town05, 10 routes) + new weather, averaged over 3 runs:**

| Method            | Driving Score ↑ | Route ↑ | Infraction ↑ | Norm. Reward ↑ |
|-------------------|-----------------|---------|--------------|----------------|
| CILRS             | 7.8 ± 0.3       | 10.3    | 76.2         | not reported   |
| LBC               | 12.3 ± 2.0      | 31.9    | 66.0         | not reported   |
| TransFuser (LiDAR)| 31.0 ± 3.6      | 47.5    | 76.8         | not reported   |
| Roach             | 41.6 ± 1.8      | 96.4    | 43.3         | 0.34 ± 0.05    |
| LAV (LiDAR)       | 46.5 ± 3.0      | 69.8    | 73.4         | not reported   |
| **MILE**          | **61.1 ± 3.2**  | 97.4    | 63.0         | **0.67 ± 0.02**|
| Expert (privileged)| 88.4 ± 0.9     | 97.6    | 90.5         | 0.70 ± 0.01    |

Headline: +31% relative driving score vs. LAV; nearly 2× the cumulative reward of Roach (same training data); approaches privileged expert. Camera-only beats LiDAR-using TransFuser and LAV.

Ablations (Table 2, same setup):
- Single frame, no 3D lifting: driving score 51.8 (vs. 61.1) — 3D-lift inductive bias matters.
- Single frame (with 3D lifting but no temporal model): 59.6 — temporal latent dynamics adds modest gain.
- No prior/posterior KL matching: cumulative reward drops 7621 → 6084 — probabilistic transition modelling matters.
- No BeV segmentation supervision: driving score 55.0 — segmentation supervision is helpful but not essential.

Deployment (Table 3): fully recurrent inference matches reset-state driving score (62.1 vs. 61.1) at 43.0 Hz vs. 6.2 Hz. Adding noise to the past latent collapses the score to 48.8, showing the recurrent state is actively used (not just discarded).

Driving in imagination: model retains comparable driving performance with up to 30% of the drive in pure imagining mode (no observations).

## Limitations & Open Questions
- Evaluated only in CARLA simulator; no real-world transfer demonstrated.
- Requires BeV semantic segmentation labels for supervision (the authors call out self-supervision as an open avenue).
- No learned reward function — paper notes inferring reward from expert data would enable explicit planning in the world model and is left open.
- Higher-dimensional spatial latents (256×12×12 and beyond) underperform the 512-d 1D latent in closed-loop driving — the prior/posterior matching does not scale gracefully with latent dimensionality.
- Single forward camera and 5 Hz sub-sampling; no multi-camera or higher-rate evaluation reported.
