# CaRL: Learning Scalable Planning Policies with Simple Rewards

## Keywords
- ppo, reinforcement-learning, route-completion, carla, nuplan, privileged-planning, scalable-rl

## TL;DR
Contemporary RL planners for driving rely on complex shaped rewards summing many additive terms (progress, position, orientation, comfort), which trap PPO in local minima as the mini-batch size is increased and prevent data-scale training. The authors propose a single-term reward — route completion multiplied by soft penalty factors and gated by hard terminal penalties — that scales gracefully with mini-batch size up to 16384. Trained on 300M CARLA samples and 500M nuPlan samples on a single 8-GPU node, the resulting CaRL policy reaches 64 DS on CARLA longest6 v2 (vs 22 for Roach, 7 for Think2Drive) and 91.3 / 90.6 CLS on nuPlan Val14 (non-reactive / reactive), while running 7-17x faster than the prior best learning baseline.

## Problem & Motivation
Privileged neural planners trained with imitation learning (IL) suffer from open-loop / closed-loop distribution shift and underperform rule-based planners. RL is a natural alternative, but recent CARLA RL planners (Roach, Think2Drive) use additive shaped rewards combining many handcrafted terms — speed, lateral position, orientation, comfort — usually computed by an embedded rule-based planner. These designs upper-bound performance to the rule-based planner's quality, introduce exploitable local minima (e.g. the Roach policy learns to wait at green lights to harvest the speed-zero reward at red lights), and crucially do not scale: increasing PPO's mini-batch size by 4x with a Roach-style reward drops performance by ~32 DS. Without scalable mini-batches, distributed-data-parallel scaling of PPO is blocked, leaving driving RL trapped at 1-10M samples while robotics breakthroughs use 100x more.

## Innovation Points
- **Single-term reward** — `r_t = RC_t * (∏ p_t) - T`: route completion is the only positive signal; soft penalties `p_t ∈ [0,1]` (speed, comfort, lane distance, time-to-collision) multiplicatively reduce it; hard penalties (collision, off-road, red-light) terminate the episode with terminal cost `T`. No rule-based planner is embedded.
- **Three reward principles** — finite total obtainable reward (caps at 100 RC, no infinite loopholes); reward only specifies what to do, not how; the reward's global optimum aligns with the DS evaluation metric.
- **Atari-style PPO hyperparameters for CARLA** — switching from CARLA-tuned (lr 1e-5, 959 off-policy steps) to Atari-tuned (lr 2.5e-4, 15 off-policy steps) trains 10 hours faster and gains +11 DS on Roach; enables higher learning rates needed for scaling.
- **Mode → mean action** — sampling the mean of the Beta action distribution at inference (not the mode) gives +1 DS and -3 std.
- **DD-PPO scaling** — uses distributed-data-parallel PPO without preemption to scale to 300M CARLA samples (mini-batch 16384) on 8x A100 + 108 EPYC cores in one week.
- **Cross-simulator transfer** — same method trained on nuPlan with 500M samples and a constant survival bonus (to handle the fixed 15-second episode after route completion).

## Model Architecture
- Input: bird's-eye-view (BEV) semantic segmentation rendered from privileged ground-truth perception.
- Backbone: a small CNN policy network (the nuPlan version is ~2M parameters; CARLA version similar / smaller than IL baselines).
- Action distribution: Beta distribution over the car's continuous actions; inference uses the distribution mean.
- Algorithm: PPO (Proximal Policy Optimization) with Atari hyperparameters; DD-PPO for parallel scaling.
- Reward: `r_t = RC_t * (∏ p_t) - T`. Soft penalties cover speed limit, comfort, lane distance, time-to-collision. Hard terminal penalties: collision (T=1), red-light (T=1), other infractions (T=0).
- Training scale: 300M samples on CARLA (1 week, 8x A100 40GB + 108 EPYC Rome cores); 500M samples on nuPlan.
- Evaluation simulators: CARLA 0.9.15 (longest6 v2, towns 1-6, 36 routes, 7 safety-critical scenario types) and nuPlan Val14 (1118 simulations, non-reactive log replay and reactive IDM traffic).

## Benchmark Results

**CARLA longest6 v2 (privileged planners):**
| Method | Type | DS ↑ | RC ↑ | Ped ↓ | Veh ↓ | MS ↓ | Time (ms) ↓ |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| PDM-Lite | Rule | 73 | 100 | 0.00 | 0.18 | 7.67 | 18 |
| PlanT | IL | 62 | 96 | 0.07 | 0.43 | 3.18 | 18 |
| Think2Drive | RL | 7 | 39 | 0.97 | 2.55 | 18.05 | 6 |
| Roach | RL | 22 | 77 | 0.45 | 2.42 | 7.12 | 7 |
| **CaRL (Ours)** | RL | **64** | **82** | **0.01** | **0.36** | **1.71** | **8** |

CaRL beats the best prior RL planner (Roach) by 42 DS and Think2Drive by 57 DS, and outperforms IL planner PlanT by 2 DS with a smaller model. PDM-Lite (which exploits leaderboard scenario metadata) still leads on DS but is 2x slower.

**nuPlan Val14 (CLS = closed-loop score):**
| Method | Type | NR CLS ↑ | NR Col ↑ | NR RC ↑ | R CLS ↑ | R Col ↑ | R RC ↑ | Time ↓ |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| PDM-Closed | Rule | 92.8 | 98.1 | 92.1 | 92.1 | 97.9 | 90.3 | 104 |
| PLUTO | IL+Rule | 92.6 | 97.9 | 93.0 | 89.7 | 97.1 | 86.1 | 237 |
| Diff. Planner | IL | 89.6 | 95.9 | 94.2 | 82.7 | 93.1 | 85.9 | 138 |
| **CaRL (Ours)** | RL | **91.3** | 97.4 | **94.4** | **90.6** | 97.1 | **91.3** | **14** |

CaRL is the strongest learning-based planner in both reactive and non-reactive settings, beating Diffusion Planner by 1.7 (NR) / 7.9 (R) and runs 7-17x faster than baselines.

**Reward × mini-batch size ablation (DS on CARLA):**
| Reward | Local Hints? | MB 256 | MB 1024 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Roach (complex) | yes | 34 ± 7 | 2 ± 2 |
| CaRL (simple) | no | 21 ± 11 | **38 ± 3** |

The complex reward collapses with larger MB; the simple reward improves.

**Data scale (CaRL):** 10M samples (MB 1024) → 31 DS / 63 RC; 300M samples (MB 16384) → 64 DS / 82 RC (+33 DS).

## Limitations & Open Questions
- Urban driving up to ~80 km/h only; high-speed highway driving not investigated.
- Sim-only; no Sim2Real demonstration on real cars.
- Trained on 7 of the leaderboard 2.0 scenario types; remaining scenario types untested.
- CARLA failure modes: missing exits on highway off-ramps, and rear-end collisions when a trailing car runs a red light (charged to the agent in CARLA scoring).
- Comfort penalty bounds set heuristically (no human reference data in CARLA physics); may not match real-vehicle comfort.
- Reward has no explicit time-urgency term — relies on PPO's discount factor.
- Method tested only with PPO (Monte-Carlo returns); may be harder to optimize with TD-based algorithms (Q-learning, SAC).
- Training only at level 4 (training and evaluation in the same towns); level 5 generalization to unseen towns not studied.
